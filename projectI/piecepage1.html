The Lovers, 1928

This piece is called The Lovers, painted with oils on linen in 1928, it forms part of a 4 series variation of the same visual theme: 2 figures, a male and a female, embracing and kissing with their heads covered in what appears to be rags. This piece, like all of Magrittes extensive work, is up for interpretation, but the main themes that appear in this painting are desire and platonic love.
