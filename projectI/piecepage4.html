The Son of Man, 1964

The Son of Man, painted with oil on canvas in 1964, is most probably his best known work. It depicts a man with a suit and a bowler hat standing in front of a short brick wall and behind it the sea and a cloudy sky, in front of his face hovers a green apple. Magritte has remarked that the subject behind this is that everything we see hides something, and that we, as humans, have the curiosity to see what is not shown to us.
