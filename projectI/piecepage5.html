Decalcomania, 1966

Decalcomania, painted in 1966, depicts, on the background, like most of his paintings, the sea with a cloudy blue sky, and on the foreground, to the left side, the back of a man in a suit and a bowler hat, and to the right a red curtain cut with the shape of the same man, exposing the aforementioned sky. Like most of Magrittes work, this painting is up for interpretation, the carved out silhouette of a man invokes a feeling of misplacement, the idea of thinking outside oneself, outside the box.
