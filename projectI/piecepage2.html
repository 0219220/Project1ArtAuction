The False Mirror, 1928

The False Mirror, being one of Magrittes most famous and appraised works, was painted with oils on linen in 1928 in Paris. It depicts the image of an eye on the foreground, with its pupil floating in the middle of a cloud filled sky. Its known mainly for being disruptive, and for causing people who see this artwork to feel uneasy, almost like an invitation to question our grasp on reality, to see the world differently.
